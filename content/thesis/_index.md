## The Thesis

AI is a disruptive force that will displace labor and force economic readjustment. The market views this purely as a productivity boom and is mispricing the tail risks: social disruption, regulatory backlash, and deflationary pressure.

**The Opportunity:** Opportunistic credit entry points in digital infrastructure and power that emerge from this dislocation ‚Äî financing physical assets with secondary market value (power generation, completed data centers) where traditional lenders are too slow or risk-averse.

**The Null Hypothesis:** *"The market is currently pricing edge risks in AI infrastructure and adoption fairly."*

Our goal is to rigorously challenge and potentially disprove this hypothesis.

## The Credit Lens

This research is written from the perspective of an opportunistic credit investor. Every module is tested against:

1. **What is the downside?** ‚Äî Collateral value, recovery rates, structural subordination
2. **What breaks first?** ‚Äî Weakest links in the capital structure chain
3. **Where is the secondary value?** ‚Äî Physical assets that retain value in dislocation scenarios (power, shells, land, fiber)
4. **What are others missing?** ‚Äî Information asymmetry between public market pricing and physical reality

Data centers and their associated power infrastructure are identifiable, recoverable assets. In a dislocation, mispriced debt backed by these assets creates the credit bid opportunity.
